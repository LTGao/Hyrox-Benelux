↩ Returning athlete re-entry point

After race day, returning athletes receive a personalised result email referencing their previous season finish time and suggesting a next goal. This re-enters them at the Interest stage with a warm, data-driven trigger rather than a cold awareness campaign. The 10.9% multi-season return rate is the current baseline. Personalised post-race flows are the lever to grow it.

New athlete acquisition

The top of the funnel is already performing. Field sizes grew 25 times in five seasons, largely driven by first-time participants entering at Awareness. The growth lever here is the quality of content and the social proof that comes from a growing doubles community sharing their experience.

Registration conversion

43% of the S8 field registered as doubles, meaning the bring-a-friend mechanic is already working organically. A structured discount programme for paired and group entries turns this organic behaviour into a measurable acquisition channel.

Retention and re-engagement

The largest growth opportunity in the funnel. 89.1% of Benelux athletes participated in only one season. A post-race personalised email flow, timed 48 to 72 hours after the finish, is the highest-ROI channel to move this number. Each percentage point recovered is thousands of additional registrations per season.

Segmentation logic

Each email in both flows is segmented by at least one variable from the race data. Sending the same message to all 52,000+ finishers wastes the data advantage. The segments that matter most:

First-timer vs returner — fundamentally different message
Finish time bucket — sub-80 / 80–100 / 100+ min
Division — solo open vs doubles vs pro
City — location-specific logistics and partners
Key timing principles

The post-race D+2 email is the most important send in the entire flow. Motivation to re-register peaks 24 to 72 hours after finishing and drops sharply after one week. Most race re-registrations happen within the first 10 days post-finish or not at all.

What this requires

Both flows assume race timing data (finish time, splits, city) is passed into the email platform on race day. With that data connected, all personalisation is automated. The flows described here can be built in any major ESP: Klaviyo, Braze, Mailchimp, or ActiveCampaign.

08 · Community Growth

The shift from solo racing to social sport

Doubles participation has grown from 24% of the Benelux field in Season 4 to 43% in Season 8. Almost half of all athletes now race with a partner. This is not just a format preference — it is a structural shift in how people experience HYROX, and it opens a direct path for community-driven growth.

Doubles share growth across 5 seasons

Doubles grew from 24% to 43% of the Benelux field over five seasons, an 18 percentage point increase. Solo open participation dropped from 56% to 50% over the same period. Athletes are actively choosing to race as a pair rather than alone.

2 in 1
Each doubles conversion is two registrations

Converting a solo participant into a doubles entry means one athlete recruits another. At the S8 field size of 28,897 participants, the 43% doubles share already represents over 12,000 athletes who came because someone brought them. That social recruitment loop is the most cost-effective acquisition channel available.

🤝

Bring a Friend Discount

A targeted post-race email to solo finishers offering a reduced doubles entry for the next event converts individual participants into recruiting agents. The athlete who signs up gets a better price. The friend they bring is a brand new participant. Both are more likely to return because their commitment is now shared.

🏆

First-Timer to Doubles Pathway

First-time open finishers are the largest pool of potential doubles recruits. A follow-up campaign that celebrates their result, shows what doubles looks like in practice, and offers a time-limited discount on paired entry meets the athlete at peak motivation, shortly after their first finish line experience.

📍

Gym and Club Partnerships

Doubles and group entries are a natural fit for gym communities, running clubs, and CrossFit boxes. A partnership programme that gives affiliated gyms a referral discount code for team entries turns local fitness communities into recurring sourcing channels, city by city across Amsterdam, Rotterdam, Maastricht, Utrecht, Heerenveen, Gent and Mechelen.

📈
The Community Flywheel

Doubles athletes recruit their partner before the race. Both athletes share the experience during the race. Both are more likely to return because the social bond with their partner carries through to the next season. The 10.9% multi-season return rate in the data is almost certainly higher among doubles participants than solo ones. Building campaigns around team formats does not just grow the field in one season — it compounds across seasons.
