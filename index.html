After race day, returning athletes receive a personalised result email referencing their previous season finish time and suggesting a next goal. This re-enters them at the Interest stage with a warm, data-driven trigger rather than a cold awareness campaign. The 10.9% multi-season return rate is the current baseline. Personalised post-race flows are the lever to grow it.

New athlete acquisition

The top of the funnel is already performing. Field sizes grew 25 times in five seasons, largely driven by first-time participants entering at Awareness. The growth lever here is the quality of content and the social proof that comes from a growing doubles community sharing their experience.

Registration conversion

43% of the S8 field registered as doubles, meaning the bring-a-friend mechanic is already working organically. A structured discount programme for paired and group entries turns this organic behaviour into a measurable acquisition channel.

Retention and re-engagement

The largest growth opportunity in the funnel. 89.1% of Benelux athletes participated in only one season. A post-race personalised email flow, timed 48 to 72 hours after the finish, is the highest-ROI channel to move this number. Each percentage point recovered is thousands of additional registrations per season.

About This Analysis

This dashboard was built as a case study for a digital marketing role application, demonstrating the ability to go from raw data to audience insight to actionable strategy. The analysis covers 74,645 participants across 17 Benelux events and 7 cities (Seasons 4 to 8, 2021 to 2026). Data was sourced from Kaggle (S4 to S6) and scraped directly from the HYROX platform (S7 to S8), including previously unreported events in Heerenveen, Gent and Mechelen. All seasons were additionally scraped for athlete names to enable cross-season retention analysis across 61,661 unique athletes.

The retention analysis is grounded in the Benelux data specifically, not benchmarks borrowed from other markets. The strategic recommendations reflect experience working in the Dutch sports industry and understanding how local fitness communities make decisions.

The shift from solo racing to social sport

Doubles participation has grown from 24% of the Benelux field in Season 4 to 43% in Season 8. Almost half of all athletes now race with a partner. This is not just a format preference — it is a structural shift in how people experience HYROX, and it opens a direct path for community-driven growth.

Doubles share growth across 5 seasons

Doubles grew from 24% to 43% of the Benelux field over five seasons, an 18 percentage point increase. Solo open participation dropped from 56% to 50% over the same period. Athletes are actively choosing to race as a pair rather than alone.

2 in 1
Each doubles conversion is two registrations

Converting a solo participant into a doubles entry means one athlete recruits another. At the S8 field size of 28,897 participants, the 43% doubles share already represents over 12,000 athletes who came because someone brought them. That social recruitment loop is the most cost-effective acquisition channel available.

🤝

Bring a Friend Discount

A targeted post-race email to solo finishers offering a reduced doubles entry for the next event converts individual participants into recruiting agents. The athlete who signs up gets a better price. The friend they bring is a brand new participant. Both are more likely to return because their commitment is now shared.

🏆

First-Timer to Doubles Pathway

First-time open finishers are the largest pool of potential doubles recruits. A follow-up campaign that celebrates their result, shows what doubles looks like in practice, and offers a time-limited discount on paired entry meets the athlete at peak motivation, shortly after their first finish line experience.

📍

Gym and Club Partnerships

Doubles and group entries are a natural fit for gym communities, running clubs, and CrossFit boxes. A partnership programme that gives affiliated gyms a referral discount code for team entries turns local fitness communities into recurring sourcing channels, city by city across Amsterdam, Rotterdam, Maastricht, Utrecht, Heerenveen, Gent and Mechelen.

📈
The Community Flywheel

Doubles athletes recruit their partner before the race. Both athletes share the experience during the race. Both are more likely to return because the social bond with their partner carries through to the next season. The 10.9% multi-season return rate in the data is almost certainly higher among doubles participants than solo ones. Building campaigns around team formats does not just grow the field in one season — it compounds across seasons.

Campaign 01 · Acquisition

From the Sidelines to the Start Line

Target: Audience ticket holders + athletes' networks

The insight

Every HYROX event sells audience tickets. These people are not random bystanders — they came to watch someone they know. They stood at the finish line. They saw the t-shirts, the timing screens, the community. Research on live sports attendance consistently shows that spectators who know a participant personally have the highest conversion rate to first-time participant of any cold audience. Yet no follow-up email exists for audience ticket buyers. That is the gap.

The hook: identity, not discount

Audience ticket holders are not price-sensitive — they already paid to watch. The lever is identity and exclusivity. Not "register now and save 10%" but "you were there at the start. Become part of it."

Mechanic 01

Audience-to-Athlete Badge

Athletes who convert from audience ticket holder to racer receive a special "Sidelines to Start Line" digital badge on their HYROX profile. Visible on the leaderboard. Shareable. Creates social proof for the next wave of spectators.

Mechanic 02

Early Bird + Priority Wave

Audience ticket buyers get 48-hour early access to the next event's registration before it opens publicly, plus priority start wave placement. Scarcity and status combined. No discount needed.

Mechanic 03

Bring a Buddy — Athlete Edition

Existing athletes receive a personalised "bring a friend" link after their race. When their friend registers, both get a small registration credit and a "Race Together" badge. Converts 1 registration into 2. The friend is a pre-warmed lead who already has social proof from someone they trust.

Mechanic 04

Gym Partnership Awareness

Partner with Basic-Fit, SportCity, and CrossFit boxes city by city. Gyms promote HYROX registration to their members via in-app banners, email, and front-desk QR codes. Affiliated gym members get a partner entry discount code. Gym gets co-branding on race day. Both sides win.

Campaign 02 · Retention

Your Next PB is One Race Away

Target: Previous Benelux finishers who have not re-registered

The insight

89% of Benelux athletes race once and do not return to a Benelux event. But most of them did not stop being athletes. They are still training, still tracking their fitness, still following HYROX on Instagram. The gap is not motivation — it is activation. Nobody reminded them at the right moment with the right message. This campaign fixes that with a single hook: your finish time, and the specific number it could become.

The hook: the PB they know is possible

Every athlete who has finished HYROX knows roughly what they could shave off their time. Data from the Benelux dataset shows that repeat athletes improve by an average of 7 to 12 minutes on their second race. That is a concrete, believable number. The campaign makes it personal: "You finished in 1:34:22. Athletes with your profile average 1:24 on their second race."

Mechanic 01

Personalised PB Projection

Email built from the athlete's actual split data. Identifies their slowest station, shows average improvement for their age group and finish-time tier, and presents a realistic target time for their second race. Data-driven and deeply personal.

Mechanic 02

Returning Athlete Badge

"Season X Veteran" badge on the race profile for athletes returning to HYROX Benelux. Visible to other athletes on the leaderboard. Simple identity signal that rewards loyalty with status rather than price.

Mechanic 03

Upgrade Path: Solo to Doubles

For solo open finishers who have not returned: offer the doubles entry as a lower-commitment re-entry point. Racing with a partner reduces the psychological barrier of "starting over alone" and converts one lapsed athlete into two new registrations.

Mechanic 04

Station-Specific Training Content

Based on the athlete's weakest split, serve a targeted 4-week training guide for that specific station. Useful, free, and keeps HYROX top of mind during the training window before next season opens.
